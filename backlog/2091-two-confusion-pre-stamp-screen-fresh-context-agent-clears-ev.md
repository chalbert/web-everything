# Two-confusion pre-stamp screen: fresh-context agent clears every fork of standard-vs-impl and merit-vs-prioritization before preparedDate

The two flaws that most often survive prep and cost deep human correction at ratification — a fork ruling on an implementation detail, and prioritization wearing a fork's clothes — are guarded today only by same-session self-assertion (the pass-4 skeptic) and a keyword tell-scan (G4). Wire a mandatory fresh-context screen: two fixed questions per fork, answered by a throwaway agent that did not author the forks, verdict recorded as a Screen: line beside each Skeptic: line; a flagged fork is rewritten before the stamp. Validated experimentally on the prepared stock (flag rate + counter-verified false-positive rate) and on a live Opus prepare run.
