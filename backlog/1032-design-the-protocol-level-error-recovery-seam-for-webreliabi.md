# Design the protocol-level error-recovery seam for webreliability

De-buries the error-recovery GAP flagged in webreliability impl epic #1019. The recovery-handler **registry** (lineage #011 / #028 / #101 / #503; built by #1051/#1052/#1053) has resolved design — but the recovery **semantics** over it (how failures are classified, retried/backed-off, surfaced, composed across handlers) is a true GAP per the #1008 triage. This card decides **how thick the Error Recovery contract is**: which concerns are normalized at the `@webeverything` contract vs. left private to handlers. Placement (contract → WE, runtime → FUI) is the standing rule, not in question.

## Grounding digest

The delegation **seam is already designed and on the project page** ([we:src/_includes/project-webreliability.njk:207-235](../src/_includes/project-webreliability.njk#L207)): `CustomRecoveryHandler.tryRecover(error, context) → RecoveryResult | null`, the first-that-accepts registry, `RecoveryOutcome = 'retry' | 'queued' | 'fallback' | 'abort'`, opaque `RecoveryResult.delay`, 1-based `RecoveryContext.attempt`, and the `recovering`/`fallback`/`failed` observable states ([we:src/_includes/project-webreliability.njk:162-185](../src/_includes/project-webreliability.njk#L162)). The `error-recovery` protocol entry ([we:src/_data/protocols.json:12-19](../src/_data/protocols.json#L12)) and the Reliability Intent's `tolerance`/`recovery`/`fallback` dimensions ([we:src/_data/intents.json:1458-1487](../src/_data/intents.json#L1458)) are the consumers the contract must serve.

This decision sits on **already-researched ground** — the [Error Recovery & Retry Patterns](/research/error-recovery-patterns/) topic — **extended for this card** ([report](../reports/2026-06-19-error-recovery-protocol-seam.md)). The original survey covered single-handler JS data-fetching libs (TanStack/axios-retry/urql/SWR/Apollo) and settled the registry layer. The extension adds the two angles it missed: **resilience composition** (Polly `PolicyWrap`, resilience4j decorators) and **recovery-state surfacing** (TanStack `status` vs `fetchStatus`, the circuit-breaker closed/open/half-open canon).

## Axis framing

Every concern decomposes on one axis: **does the contract normalize the cross-handler *output* the consumer/UX reads, or is the *mechanism* private to each handler?** The page is currently inconsistent — it normalizes some outputs (`RecoveryOutcome`, the observable states) but lists "Error classification" as `✅ handler` ([we:src/_includes/project-webreliability.njk:52](../src/_includes/project-webreliability.njk#L52)) and leaves the active-recovery vocabulary loose (its own open question #3, [we:src/_includes/project-webreliability.njk:283](../src/_includes/project-webreliability.njk#L283)). The spine that resolves it, grounded in the report: **normalize the outputs every consumer/UX needs regardless of which handler ran; keep every per-operation mechanism in handlers** (adapter-as-normalization-hub). That makes two concerns genuine forks (classification *disposition* and recovery *phase* — both are cross-handler outputs the UX reads, and a coherent "leave it private" branch exists for each), and two concerns forced invariants (backoff math is a mechanism → handler; composition order is author-specific → composite handler, not protocol orchestration).

## Fork 1 — failure classification: normalized disposition, or handler-private?

*Fork exists because* both branches are coherent end-states: a thin disposition vocabulary the UX reads is a real design, and "no contract vocabulary at all, every handler classifies privately" is also a real design (it is roughly today's page state). They cannot both be the contract — either the disposition is in it or it isn't.

The original survey settled the *mechanism*: raw error→cause classification (HTTP status codes, `Retry-After`, DB error shapes) is operation-specific and stays in handlers. What's open is whether the contract carries the **output** of classification — a normalized disposition the consumer/UX reads without inspecting the raw error.

- **(A — recommended) Contract carries a normalized disposition** — `transient` (worth recovering) · `terminal` (give up) · `deferred` (queued for later) — that handlers map raw errors into; the raw classification logic stays private. This is the adapter-as-normalization-hub pattern (normalize the cross-handler output, not the impl): without it, every consumer re-derives "is this recoverable" from the raw error or from handler identity, coupling the UX to specific handlers. **Correction (red-team):** disposition is **orthogonal to**, *not* 1:1 with, the Reliability Intent `tolerance` dimension. `tolerance` (`forgivable`/`degraded`/`terminal`, [we:src/_data/intents/reliability.json:7-12](../src/_data/intents/reliability.json#L7)) measures **user-impact severity** ("how badly is the user affected"); disposition measures **recoverability/timing** ("should we retry / give up / defer"). They share only the token `terminal`, and even there the semantics differ (intent = "can't render"; disposition = "stop retrying"). The UX reads **both** axes together — a `forgivable`+`transient` failure (silent retry) is not a `forgivable`+`terminal` one (toast, no retry). So disposition earns its place as a distinct axis, not as a re-skin of `tolerance`. *Residual:* risks a premature three-value enum — mitigated by keeping it an open meta-schema (handlers may carry a richer private classification; the disposition is only the UX-facing projection).
- **(B) Fully handler-private classification.** Maximum flexibility, zero premature vocabulary — but then the UX cannot reason about "is this recoverable" generically; every consumer re-derives it from the raw error or from handler identity, which couples the UX to specific handlers. Rejected: it pushes a genuinely cross-handler output into private space.

## Fork 2 — recovery surfacing: a standardized phase discriminator, or consumer-defined?

*Fork exists because* the page today has only the coarse `recovering`/`fallback`/`failed` triple and explicitly flags the gap ("how should Resource Loader surface retrying vs. queued vs. circuit-open?", [we:src/_includes/project-webreliability.njk:283](../src/_includes/project-webreliability.njk#L283)). "Standardize a phase enum" and "leave the vocabulary to each consumer" are both coherent and mutually exclusive at the contract.

TanStack Query is the precedent: it splits `status` (`pending`/`error`/`success` — the data) from `fetchStatus` (`fetching`/`paused`/`idle` — the recovery/network phase), so an offline-and-waiting query is `success`+`paused`, not a new error state. The circuit-breaker canon adds `circuit-open` as a *phase*, not a terminal failure (the next probe may recover).

- **(A — recommended) Contract adds a recovery-phase discriminator** on the in-flight `recovering` state, emitted as an observable the Reliability + Loader Intent UX binds to, without coupling to which handler ran. Keeps the phase vocabulary *separate* from the data/loader status (the TanStack split). **Scope (red-team amendment):** ship it as an **open meta-schema with a minimal core** — `retrying` (attempt pending, possibly after `delay`) · `queued` (handler owns deferred replay) · `awaiting-manual` (user-triggered retry offered) — and register `circuit-open` (probing-gated) as an **extension value, not a core member**. The protocol ships **no** circuit-breaker handler yet (it is a *planned* impl, [we:src/_includes/project-webreliability.njk:277](../src/_includes/project-webreliability.njk#L277)), so baking `circuit-open` into core would bias the contract toward one resilience pattern. *Residual:* the set may grow (e.g. `rate-limited`) — same path: register as an extension, promotion is a status flip, not a re-survey. This is the standardize-the-meta-schema-not-the-list rule.
- **(B) Leave the active-recovery vocabulary to each consumer.** Each app invents its own retrying/queued/open distinction. Coherent but weak: the surfacing vocabulary is inherently cross-handler (the UX needs one regardless of handler), so leaving it private re-creates the exact fragmentation the protocol exists to remove. Rejected.

## Fork 3 — composition across handlers: single-dispatch composite, or protocol orchestration?

*Fork exists because* under strict first-that-accepts, only one handler owns recovery — so getting circuit-breaker **+** retry **+** offline-queue to cooperate on one operation requires *either* an author-written composite handler *or* a protocol-level model where a handler can hand off to the next (e.g. a `'continue'` outcome). Both are coherent contracts; they are mutually exclusive in what `RecoveryResult` means.

The canonical resilience libraries decide this cleanly. Polly `PolicyWrap` and resilience4j decorators both make multi-concern cooperation an **explicit, author-ordered wrap** — and the order is load-bearing: Polly's own guidance is `CircuitBreaker`-wraps-`Retry` for short delays but `WaitAndRetry`-wraps-`CircuitBreaker` for long delays. *Both orderings are correct in different timing regimes.*

- **(A — recommended) Single-dispatch stays; cooperation is an author-composed composite handler** — a `CustomRecoveryHandler` that itself holds and orders sub-handlers. The protocol stays first-that-accepts (consistent with the ratified registry-not-chain decision, [we:src/_includes/project-webreliability.njk:239](../src/_includes/project-webreliability.njk#L239)). A baked protocol order would have to pick breaker-wraps-retry *or* retry-wraps-breaker, and Polly proves both are right in different regimes — so baking either is wrong; the author owns the order via the composite (the direct `PolicyWrap` analogue: a handler that *nests* sub-handlers). *Residual (the red-team target):* this pushes a recurring need (breaker-wrapping-retry) onto every app. If it proves constant, promote a minimal `'continue'`/pass-through outcome later — a status-flip add, not a contract break. **Why the promotion path is NOT the Polly answer (red-team, record so it isn't reopened on the same evidence):** a `'continue'` outcome expresses only *flat fallthrough* (try A, else B, else C). Polly's regime-dependent cases need **nesting** — retry executing *inside* the breaker's call so the breaker counts the retried attempt as one — which a flat continuation cannot represent; only the nesting composite can. So `'continue'` is the weaker primitive for a different need (linear escalation), not the composition model B claims; the composite remains correct for the load-bearing Polly case.
- **(B) Optional `'continue'` outcome for flat escalation.** `RecoveryResult` gains a way to say "I acted, continue to the next handler." **Not exclusive with A** (red-team reframe): it's *additive and backward-compatible* — the registry stays first-that-accepts, apps opt in by returning `'continue'`, apps that never do get pure single-dispatch unchanged. And it serves a **different need than A** — flat escalation (try A, then B), not the nesting composites handle. So this is not a competing contract shape to reject; it's a **deferred optional dimension** (dimension-vs-fixed-mechanic: flat escalation is a legitimate end-state, just a *different* one). **Deferred on prioritization, not merit** — YAGNI/minimal-surface: don't add the outcome until a real app demonstrates the flat-escalation need (timing is prioritization, not a fork). *Build-time residual to watch:* keep the opt-in `'continue'` clean of the registry-not-chain objection (it must stay a per-result signal over a registry, never a middleware chain object).

## Supported by default (not forks — forced invariants)

- **Backoff/retry math stays in handlers; the contract carries only the resolved opaque `delay` (ms).** The original survey is unambiguous (backoff is operation-specific; jitter is opt-in best-practice for distributed HTTP, overkill for local compute). "Protocol owns backoff math" is the *excluded* branch — it prescribes a strategy that doesn't fit every operation, for no interop gain. `RecoveryResult.delay` already carries the output ([we:src/_includes/project-webreliability.njk:213-217](../src/_includes/project-webreliability.njk#L213)).
- **Placement: contract types → `@webeverything`, runtime handlers → FUI.** The standing constellation rule ([we:docs/agent/platform-decisions.md](../docs/agent/platform-decisions.md)), not re-litigated here. The disposition/phase enums + the `CustomRecoveryHandler`/`RecoveryResult`/`RecoveryContext` types are WE; every concrete handler (HTTP retry, circuit breaker, offline queue) and the resolution runtime is FUI (#1052).

## Ruling — ratified 2026-06-19 (A / A / A, amended)

Ratified all three forks at **A**, with red-team amendments. A throwaway skeptic sub-agent was run (prompted only to refute); it confirmed Fork 3 but landed two corrections on Forks 1–2, applied above.

- **Fork 1 — A (~80%): contract carries a normalized failure disposition** (`transient`/`terminal`/`deferred`); raw classification stays in handlers. **Amended:** the "1:1 with the Intent `tolerance` axis" rationale was a **mis-citation** and is struck — disposition (recoverability/timing) is **orthogonal** to `tolerance` (user-impact severity, `forgivable`/`degraded`/`terminal`, [we:src/_data/intents/reliability.json:7-12](../src/_data/intents/reliability.json#L7)); they share only the token `terminal` with differing meaning. Disposition ratifies on its own merit (a cross-handler output the UX reads), as a *second* axis the UX consumes alongside `tolerance`, kept as an open meta-schema.
- **Fork 2 — A (~75%): contract adds a recovery-phase discriminator** on `recovering`. **Amended scope:** ratified as an **open meta-schema, core = `retrying`/`queued`/`awaiting-manual`**; `circuit-open` (and any future `rate-limited`) are **registered extension values, not core** — the protocol ships no circuit-breaker handler yet, so core stays pattern-neutral.
- **Fork 3 — A (~80%, raised from Med): single-dispatch + author-composed composite handler** is the **forced floor** (the nesting composite ≈ `PolicyWrap` is the correct analogue for Polly's regimes; `'continue'` is *flat fallthrough* and cannot express nesting). **Reframe (red-team, 2nd pass):** B is **not a rejected competing shape** — it's a *deferred optional, app-opt-in capability* for flat escalation (an additive, backward-compatible `'continue'` outcome; apps that don't use it get pure single-dispatch). It covers a *different* need than A, so it's a dimension to add later, not a branch that lost. **Deferred on prioritization (YAGNI/minimal-surface), not merit** — ship it when an app proves the flat-escalation need, keeping the opt-in clean of the registry-not-chain objection.
- **Invariants (unchanged):** backoff math → handlers, contract carries only opaque `delay`; placement contract types → `@webeverything`, runtime handlers → FUI.

**What this graduates:** a thin **semantics layer over the existing `error-recovery` seam** — two normalized output vocabularies (disposition + recovery-phase, both open meta-schemas) + the single-dispatch/composite composition rule. No new entity file; the contract types land in `@webeverything` and the page/protocol copy updates under the webreliability impl epic #1019 via follow-up build **#1154** (scaffolded at close-out — it authors the disposition + recovery-phase enums into `we:reliability/contract.ts` + the `error-recovery` protocol entry and reconciles the project page). Unblocks the held error-recovery impl slice.
