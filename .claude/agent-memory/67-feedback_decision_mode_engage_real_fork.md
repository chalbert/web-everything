When presenting a decision (especially `/next decision` mode), do **not** collapse the genuine
tension into "ratify N of M sub-calls as written" with one or two leftover questions. Surface the
real fork and take a position on it.

**Why:** On #272 (guard protocol) I framed the structural keystone + one enum as open and waved
through naming/provider/grounding as "ratify as written." The user pushed back — *"not mostly
endorsing, not bit decision."* The actual call wasn't "Protocol vs one-big-intent"; it was whether
the unification was **load-bearing or a coincidental shape-match** (it was half-real: protocolize
the provider+predicate seam, leave deny-outcomes per-member). That tension was hidden by my
pre-settling.

**How to apply:** Lead with the single hardest question and argue a real stance (not a rubber
stamp). Treat my "ratify as written" items with suspicion — if a sub-call has any genuine tradeoff,
present it as open with options + a reasoned pick, per planning-as-discussion. Decision-mode is a
call to *make*, not a checklist to *approve*. Pairs with [[feedback_plain_language_review_checklists]]
and [[feedback_authoring_standard_workflow]].
