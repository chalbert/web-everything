Monetization/open-core tiering decisions get a **lighter, explicitly-revisitable treatment** than WE standards. A *standard* must be strong and stable; a *monetization* ruling is **soft-accepted** and re-tuned as usage data accrues and the market evolves.

**Why:** the market and what counts as "premium" shift; over-committing a price line as if it were a permanent contract is brittle and wastes deliberation.

**How to apply:**
- Fix the **partitioning principle** firmly (the rule that generalizes — e.g. #775's cost/hosting line: free = deterministic/local/fixed-cost; paid = per-call-cost OR hosted/credential-holding). Treat **specific knob placements** as provisional, revisitable with evidence.
- **Never price on a shifting category.** "Proprietary vs open" is unstable (licenses change, vendors can open a format) — a brittle basis for a durable line. Draw the line on a **structural** property that doesn't drift (does it cost us per-use? do we host / hold credentials?). #775 Fork 2 resolved to the cost/hosting axis over the proprietary one for exactly this reason; the market's "Figma = Pro" gate is on *hosted sync*, not on a local file parse.
- Stamp decision items with a "monetization ≠ standard (soft-accepted, revisitable)" read-me note so future turns inherit the lighter treatment.
