A decision-mode alternative you **generate mid-discussion** and propose to ratify *in place of* the
prepared bold default enters ratification with **less** scrutiny than the default it replaces — the prep
ran design-first research + a fork-existence pass; your supersession ran on intuition, under cover of
"we discussed it." So the [[feedback_decision_mode_engage_real_fork]] red-team gate is **asymmetric** unless
you fix it: run the *same* attack on the **branch you're about to ratify**, including a self-raised flip.

**Why:** the near-miss this distills (#1407 Fork 2): a mid-discussion proposal to promote scroll-spy's
argmax into a first-class `viewport-presence` "most-present" selection mode *sounded* "more complete /
reliable." The merit re-derivation reversed it — the part that recurs *and* bugs out (last-section rule,
tie-break, activation offset) is **UX policy** the intent's charter keeps with the consumer, not shared
mechanism; standardizing it would bake one UX winner (violates most-flexible-default). The prepared
composition default held. It took a user "make sure it's merit based" push to catch it — nothing mechanical
fails here, because a chat-only stance leaves no on-disk trace to gate.

**How to apply:** treat **"more complete / more reliable / more robust"** as an explicit trigger phrase —
completeness bias completes the *wrong layer* as easily as the right one. Before ratifying a discussion-born
flip, re-run the per-fork classification against the *new* branch, especially **mechanism-vs-UX-policy** and
[[feedback_impl_is_not_a_standard]]: a flip that adds **standard vocabulary** for what is actually **consumer
policy** (or impl) is the classic miss. Attack fails → ratify; lands → revert/amend. Codified in
`docs/agent/backlog-workflow.md → Red-team the default`. Pairs with [[feedback_dimension_vs_fixed_mechanic]]
and [[feedback_most_flexible_default]].
