For a **standard** (the intent/vocabulary layer), once an intent's existence is warranted on merit,
design its dimension's **full member-set up front** — don't admit members one-real-consumer-at-a-time.
A named-but-incomplete dimension is an outlier waiting to break the contract; designing all members now
proves the modifier/intersect/composition rules against the whole space instead of retrofitting (a
breaking change) when a late member appears.

**Why:** the platform's value IS the coherence of the whole space. Having all the design in place early
matures the platform and prevents finding a late outlier. "Only one member is realized today" is
**demand-gating a merit question**, which [[feedback_judge_on_pure_merit_never_demand]] forbids.

**How to apply:** scope it tightly against [[project_thin_container_graduation]]-style 2nd-consumer
triggers — those govern minting a new *implementation artifact* whose reuse is unproven (demand-gated);
this governs a *standard's vocabulary completeness* whose members are already **proven & specifiable from
prior art** (merit-gated). Completeness is bounded by what incumbents prove, NOT by imagination — don't
invent speculative members with no prior art. Worked example: #1463 reversed a prepared NOT-YET (hold
`region-select` until a 2nd recognizer shape) to GO — `shape: rect|lasso|polygon|nearest` all ship in
incumbents and are specifiable now, so the dimension ships whole. Codified:
docs/agent/platform-decisions.md#vocabulary-completeness-early. See also
[[feedback_dimension_vs_fixed_mechanic]].
