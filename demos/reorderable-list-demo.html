Reorderable List Playground

The runnable home for the Reorderable List block — the order-is-user-mutable half of "drag and drop", realizing the reorder intent. The headline here is the keyboard path, which is part of the contract, not an add-on: a roving tabindex over the items plus the grab-then-move model (Space to grab, arrows to move, Space/Enter to drop, Escape to cancel), each move announced through a polite live region. Each card pairs a key sequence with the order it leaves behind, renders the list at that state, and runs the same audit the CI conformance suite asserts. The last card is live: drag items with the pointer, or grab and move them with the keyboard — items relocate with the atomic Element.moveBefore().

A green badge means the rendered list satisfies the verified contract: a native <ul reorderable role="listbox"> of role="option" items, each with a stable data-reorder-id and preserve-on-move, the DOM order matching the order model, exactly one item at tabindex="0" — the focused item — and every other at -1 (the roving tabindex), with the grabbed item marked data-reorder-grabbed (never the deprecated aria-grabbed). The reducer, renderer, announcer, and audit are the one shared source the CI suite runs. The renderer here is a deterministic reference; concrete strategies (a real persistence backend, cross-list groups, virtualization) live in Frontier UI.
