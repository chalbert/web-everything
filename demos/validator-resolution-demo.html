Validator Resolution Playground

A live test surface for the runtime customValidatorResolution plug (#224) — the async sibling of the validity-merge plane (#215). A real <async-validator-field> drives an async check whose answer resolves on a controllable delay, so you can fire overlapping checks and watch which answer wins.

The concept is a race: it only shows its value when answers arrive out of order. Fire a slow check, then a fast one — the slow, now-stale answer arrives last. Watch the resolution strategy reconcile it: versioning lets the stale request finish but drops its late answer; cancellation aborts the in-flight request outright. Swap the strategy below with zero field edits — that is the #004 OP-1 rule: vary the stale-answer policy, never the pending{version} → valid|invalid surface.

The surviving answer feeds the async source of a composed <validity-merge-field> (#215), so the merged :user-invalid styling reacts live — the two validation planes working together, exactly as a real form would wire them.
